Paddling Kayaks

White Water vs. Sea Kayaks

Each of these are uniquely designed for the circumstances. White water kayaks are built for maneuverability and sea kayaks are built for stability and speed.
  • White water have rounded hulls for easier contact with the water
  • White water kayaks are also shorter in length and have minimal chines, making it easier for the paddler to maneuver through fast flowing water
  • Sea kayaks have flat hulls and long chines for better stability, making them much faster than white water kayaks
  • Sea kayaks are built to give the paddler more distance per stroke

Different Types of Kayaks

  1. Creek Boats- specifically meant for fast and quicker turns, making it ideal for rivers and creeks
  2. Play Boats- meant for surfing waves, performing tricks, and holes in high-performance water sports
  3. Downriver Boats- built as a mix between Creek and Play boats, offering the paddler a controlled cruise as well as basic play features. Perfect for large rivers, bays, or lakes
  4. Sit on Top Kayaks- built for maneuverability and quick speeds, perfect for fishing, sight-seeing, or cruising
  5. Inflatable Kayaks- built only for recreational use of paddling on flat water. They are easy to pack and inflate, very stable, and can fit 2-3 people
